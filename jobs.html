How to apply

To apply for one of these jobs, ask a question, or suggest a new role for yourself, send an email to operations@pdap.io. Please include at least one of these: resume, LinkedIn profile, personal website. If you write code, please link to your GitHub profile.

Equal Opportunity Employment

We are an equal opportunity employer, and make hiring decisions based on merit. We believe that valuing diversity and inclusiveness of as many traits as possible will help us build the strongest possible community and products. People who have been impacted by the criminal legal system are especially encouraged to apply.

Context

Our mission is to help people locate, understand, and share public records about every U.S. police system.

Our project has been in motion since 2020, putting down roots in Pittsburgh as a non-profit. We received grant funding in July of 2022, and went from all-volunteer to a small paid staff. We are entering our second funded year from July 2023–July 2024.

Open-source Facilitator

Remote, part-time

This foundational software role is for an experienced software developer. You will work closely with our Executive Director, who is responsible for the product roadmap. Your will be responsible for the stewardship of our open-source code repositories.

Responsibilities

  • Review community Pull Requests, and work with the community to refine submissions.
  • Set quality standards and best practices for submitted code.
  • Refine our existing infrastructure to be more contributor-friendly.
  • Contribute technical guidance to work requirements.

Time commitment

This is a long-term engagement with flexible hours depending on your availability. As our organization grows, we may expand the role to full-time or bring on additional facilitators.

Compensation

$40 per hour minimum, scaled based on experience. Our annual budget for this role is $30,000–75,000 depending on the applicant's skills, availability, and interest in writing code.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Demonstrated expertise in publishing Python apps.
  • Excited about helping contributors learn and thrive.
  • Aligned with the collaborative ethos of open-source software development.

Software development opportunities

Remote, part-time

We are looking for skilled applicants of any experience level to work on a project basis. During an initial interview, we will discuss which projects on the roadmap seem like a good fit for your skills.

When you apply, please let us know if you're interested in a particular project.

Responsibilities

  • Submit code toward one or more of the issues on the roadmap.
  • Write tests for new code, and help with documentation.

Time commitment

These are flexible short-term fellowships, internships, or contracts. They may last a few weeks, up to 6 months depending on the project and your availability.

Compensation

$25 per hour minimum, scaled based on experience. Our annual budget for this work is $50,000–100,000.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Demonstrate basic knowledge of Python.
  • Excited about our mission.
  • Comfortable with feedback and iteration.

Development Director

Remote, part-time

Our baseline team is grant-funded, but we need more individual donor support to build capacity.

Responsibilities

  • Plan and execute fundraising campaigns and strategy.
  • Manage relationships and communication with donors.
  • Support volunteer fundraising and networking efforts.

Time commitment

This is a long-term engagement with flexible hours depending on your experience level and availability.

Compensation

$25 per hour minimum, depending on experience. Our annual budget for this work is $15,000–20,000.

Characteristics of strong applicants

  • Proven experience raising funds for non -profit projects.
  • An ethical outreach strategy which goes beyond social media.
  • An established network of foundations and potential donors.
